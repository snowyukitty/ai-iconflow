## Summary
Bargain Hunter finds used-goods deals. The category clichés are a magnifying
glass, a shopping cart, or a downward price arrow — all generic, and the
magnifier silhouette collides with system Search (L9). The shipped mark makes
the **container itself the object**: a chunky price tag, tilted -11°, in a
violet→pink brand gradient, with a bold upright white ¥ inside. The signature
device is the punched string-hole (a `fill-rule:evenodd` cut). The tag is a
specific, ownable silhouette that survives 16px on both tray and tab, and the ¥
is legible from ~24px up.

*Owner-curated reference exemplar. Retro-recorded from the shipped SVG to seed
the success canon; scores are the owner's judgement of the final mark.*

## What failed first
No failed pass to report; recorded as a positive standard.

- Why it beats the obvious concept: a search-glass would read as "search app"
  at every size no matter the color (L9). A tag reads as *price / deal* — the
  job, not the category noun (L2).
- The one opaque glyph (¥) sits **inside** the one dominant shape (the tag),
  never crossing it, so nothing fuses at small sizes (L1).
- Watch item: the -11° tilt is close to the L17 caution. It survives here
  because the tag's edges are thick and the ¥ stays axis-aligned and upright —
  the tilt styles the *container*, not the idea-carrying glyph.
