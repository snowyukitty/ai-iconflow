## Summary
fado's site needed a favicon and tray mark. The obvious category pulls were a
wine glass, a grape cluster, or a script "F" on a wine-red tile. The shipped
mark instead **fuses the initial into the domain object**: the F's stem is a
rounded ceramic plate and its two arms are stacked plates seen in perspective,
finished with one amber dish and a thin steam arc. Dark-green plates on a warm
cream outline read as *tableware first, "F" second* — a specific restaurant, not
a generic monogram. This is why the owner rates it well above ordinary letter
icons: the letter is doing object work, not decoration.

*Owner-curated reference exemplar. Retro-recorded from the shipped SVG to seed
the system's success canon; the scores are the owner's judgement of the final
mark, not a fresh first-pass measurement, so `iterations` is nominal.*

## What failed first
There is no failed pass to report — the value of this case is **why it works**,
as a positive standard for the distinctiveness gate:

- In the visual-silhouette strip the plate stack stays ownable, where a plain
  "F" tile would collapse to a generic letter-on-square (the exact shape the
  owner calls 平庸 / mediocre).
- One thing to watch at 16px: the arms sit close to the stem, so the counters
  between the plates are the first thing to confirm in the pixel zoom; the
  38-unit cream outline is what keeps them from fusing (cf. L1, L15).
- The mark ships on transparent, so on a dark tab the cream outline carries the
  contrast — legible, but the axis with the least margin.
