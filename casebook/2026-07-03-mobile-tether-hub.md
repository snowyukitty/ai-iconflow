## Summary
Windows tray/dashboard app that monitors and guards USB mobile tethering. Brief
essence "tether"; brand colors pulled from the app's own validated dark palette
(blue #3987e5 mark / aqua #199e70 accent on dark tile). Three concepts baked off:
(A) usage-ring whose end becomes a cable+plug, (B) letterform "T" whose stem ends
in a USB plug, (C) phone→PC linked rectangles. B won: legible at 16px in one
glance, specific to "tether", and the aqua plug foot gives it an ownable
two-color signature. Refinement pass enlarged the mark ~7%, added a
negative-space USB slot in the plug (reads ≥32px, fades at 16px) and a subtle
tile hairline for dark-on-dark edges.

## What failed first
Concept A (ring + cable tail) — intended as a dual reading of the app's data
gauge + tether cable — read as a **magnifying glass / search icon** at every
size in the bake sheet; the plug block fused with the cable into a "handle"
below 64px. No amount of styling fixed the silhouette collision, so the concept
was killed rather than iterated. Concept C turned to blob soup at 16–32px
(outline monitor + curved cable + filled phone = three weights fighting).
