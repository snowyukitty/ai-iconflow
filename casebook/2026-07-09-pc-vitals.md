## Summary
PC Vitals is a Windows system-tray hardware monitor (CPU/GPU temps, network ↓↑,
disk, VPN). Brief essence = **pulse** — the machine's live heartbeat, not the
category noun "monitor". Winner: a flat-geometric teal heartbeat spike on a cool
charcoal squircle, with a detached mint "live-cursor" node. It beat a full ECG
trace (muddy at 16px) and a node+signal-arcs mark (collided with sound/broadcast
icons). The single bold spike holds 16px legibility while the asymmetry + leading
node keep the silhouette ownable; teal-on-charcoal ties it to the app dashboard
palette. Shipped as the exe `.ico`, the recolor-by-state tray glyph (same
geometry, redrawn in GDI+ so they can't drift), and a verified desktop shortcut.

## What failed first
Concept A (a full ECG: pre-dip Q + tall R + undershoot S + trailing node) looked
great at 128px but its several small vertices fused into a faint squiggle at
16–24px → legibility 3, and the visual-silhouette column was nearly blank. The
node+arcs alternative (B) read clearly but its silhouette = sound/broadcast/wifi,
the wrong meaning (L9). Fix: drop to ONE bold peak plus a single undershoot
(removed the pre-dip), lifting 16px legibility to 4 while preserving the heartbeat
read; keep the node detached as the only accent so it never overlaps the stroke
(L1).
