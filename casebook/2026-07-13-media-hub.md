## Summary
A media-aggregator app whose favicon shipped as a bold white "H" with a corner
hub-node accent on a pink→violet tile. It clears every mechanical `check` and
scored distinctiveness 4, yet it is the house's canonical **monogram trap** (see
LEARNINGS L16 / L22): a bare letter on a gradient tile names nothing specific. It
is kept here as a cautionary contrast to fado's plate-"F" — *legible is not the
same as distinctive*.

## What failed first
- The first pass fused a play-triangle into the H bar; at 16px it read as a skip
  button (silhouette collision, L9). Pulling the corner hub-node off the letter
  kept the H readable (legibility 4→5).
- That fix was mechanically correct but did not address the real problem: the
  concept is a monogram. No accent placement makes a lettered tile specific — the
  distinctive move would have been an object-based concept (name-the-thing test).
