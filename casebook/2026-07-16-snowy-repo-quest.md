## Summary
Snowy Repo Quest gamifies repos and tasks into a quest board. A bare "S"
monogram, a compass, or a map pin would all be generic. The shipped mark is a
**faceted cut gemstone** — a pentagonal crown plus pavilion split into six
triangular facets in teal and gold, on a cream rounded tile, with one detached
amber quest spark. The multi-facet cut gives it an ownable, jewel-specific
silhouette (loot / reward), and the 14-unit dark-teal facet outlines keep the
triangles legible when the gem shrinks.

*Owner-curated reference exemplar. Retro-recorded from the shipped SVG to seed
the success canon; scores are the owner's judgement of the final mark.*

## What failed first
No failed pass to report; recorded as a positive standard.

- Why it works: the facets are a *specific object* (a cut gem), so the
  blacked-out silhouette is distinctive where an "S" would be nothing. This is
  the case the owner contrasts against mediocre S/H monograms.
- The facets share one cut angle and one outline weight, so they read as
  intentional geometry, not noise — the craft that separates a gem from a
  random polygon cluster.
- Watch item: six facets is near the detail ceiling for 16px; the outline
  weight (14 units ≈ a hair over one pixel at 64px) is what holds them apart.
  Any more facets and the pixel zoom would grey out (cf. §5 16px test).
