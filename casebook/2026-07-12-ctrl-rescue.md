## Summary
A non-admin Windows Desktop rescue shortcut needed to remain recognizable when a
stuck Ctrl key made typing impossible. The bake-off compared a hinged clamp, an
upload-like breakout key, and separating contacts. The hinged C-clamp won because
its mechanical release verb survived at 16px, while the two angled jaws and escaped
amber keycap made it more specific than a plain letter C.

## What failed first
The breakout-key finalist was the most literal, but its blacked-out visual shape
collapsed into a generic upload icon. The separating-contacts finalist was more
distinctive at 128px but fragmented into bracket noise at 16px. The first clamp
pass kept the correct silhouette but its 116-unit accent was barely one pixel in
some 16px raster positions. Promoting the clamp and enlarging the escaped keycap to
128 units produced a stable two-pixel amber cue and raised legibility, balance,
scalability, and craft without adding detail.

## Lessons

- [x] For emergency shortcuts, show the recovery verb as one bold mechanical silhouette; a single two-pixel escaped accent can explain release without tiny text.
