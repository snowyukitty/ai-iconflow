## Summary
Five subpage favicons needed more cuteness without confusing the creator with their rabbit mascot. A pointed late-night chapel-window crop beat a generic rounded avatar and a noisy lace rosette; every variant keeps one face geometry while page hue and expression identify the room.

## What failed first
The rounded-tile candidate kept both eyes readable at 16 px, but its outer
silhouette was indistinguishable from a stock avatar. The lace-rosette
candidate gained personality at 128 px and then collapsed into edge noise at
16 px. The chapel-window winner fixed both issues with one pointed frame and a
higher face crop. Its first authored master still triggered a craft warning:
the 10 px hairpin outline could disappear at favicon size. Raising the outline
to 26 px and the tiny hairpin mouth to 24 px removed the warning without
changing the large-size expression.

## Lessons
<!-- One reusable rule per bullet. `- [ ]` = not yet distilled into the docs;
     flip to `- [x]` after promoting it (see docs/EVOLUTION.md). -->
- [x] At 16 px, give a character favicon an ownable frame silhouette before adding more facial detail.
- [x] For a page family, lock identity geometry and vary only one expression plus one page hue.
- [x] A real costume accessory can reinforce identity, but generic rabbit ears can turn the person into the mascot.
