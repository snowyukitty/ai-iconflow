## Summary
Seed case, reconstructed from the worked example in `docs/CONCEPTING.md` and
`examples/README.md` (scores are best-effort from the session narrative). The
obvious category marks (gamepad, pixel alien, controller button, expo badge)
described "game show" generically. The stronger essence was **route** — the
site is a practical plan for tickets, lodging, and on-site decisions. Three
finalists were baked off: a notched ticket with a route slash, a five-day
gate, and a folded map whose white route doubles as a `T`. The folded map won
because its silhouette stayed ownable while the route encoded the site's job.

## What failed first
- First pass kept fold seam lines, a secondary rail, and small labels — at
  16px they turned the mark to grey soup (legibility 3, scalability 3).
- The five-day-gate finalist collapsed into a plain `T`/gate silhouette at
  small sizes — distinctive in concept, generic in shape.
- Fix: remove all internal detail except one amber checkpoint dot, enlarge
  the mark within the safe area, keep exactly one foreground idea.

## Lessons
- [x] For content-heavy guides, choose the user's job (route, decide, scout) over the category noun.
- [x] In the bake-off, prefer the candidate whose black silhouette is ownable, even if another looks more literal in color at 128px.
- [x] After choosing the winner, simplify once more for 16px: remove seam lines, tiny labels, secondary rails, decorative dots unless one is the signature accent.
- [x] If a proper name is long, fuse one initial/idea into the geometry and let the title carry the text.
